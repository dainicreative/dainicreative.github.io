A Sense of Light and Darkness

Project 1: Photos

And God said, "Let there be light," and there was light. God saw that the light was good, and He separated the light from the darkness. God called the light "day," and the darkness he called "night."

Impressionism: Use a camera to discover beauty around your life created by light and darkness. God gave us light and darkness to see His creations and He reveals in light.

This project can be interpreted as literally light and darkness, present and past, or good and evil as you imagine. Artistic and historic reference for this project is Impressionism in which artist extensively examined and experimented the use of light. With a series of motion images, create Impressionistic style works.

A Sense of Place

Project 2: Photo Manipulation

And God said, "Let the water under the sky be gathered to one place, and let dry ground appear." And it was so. God called the dry ground "land," and the gathered waters he called "seas."

Cubism: Use multiple images, frames, and layers to create a Cubist style work. Create a still life or landscape that features many different angles and planes. Begin with your original videos. Other images can be appropriated from the Internet or images that you have taken. Explore changing the scale, color and position of layers.Experiment and explore the narrative possibility of places, while trying to create a cohesive, captivating videos. Artistic and historic reference for this project is Cubism in which artist deconstructed and reconstructed object to understand and express truthfully.
